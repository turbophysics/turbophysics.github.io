About Me

Hi, my name is Sohail. I'm a USMC veteran, first gen American, art/film/literature/dog/biking/oxford comma enthusiast, and Computer Science student at Texas State University. I put this site together to showcase some of the more interesting work I've done during my attendance there.

I was born in San Antonio, but graduated highschool right down the road in New Braunfels. During my enlistment in the Marine Corps, I was stationed on MCAS New River in Jacksonville, NC, where I worked as a technician CH-53E Super Stallion avionics. I was also given fleet assistance duties of Military Police, logistician, and Tool Control Program Manager. I deployed only once, to Camp Lemonnier in Djibouti.

I chose Texas State because I felt like I had been away from home for long enough and believed this school had the most to offer culturally among schools in the immediate area. In addition to my studies, I've been able to use the resources there to springboard my hobbies. In 2017 a was a featured artist twice in student exhibitions. A charcoal portrait of my father was even awarded "Best Use of Theme." I've also been able to enroll my canine pal (Stamper P. Dog) in training courses with the hopes of getting him into agility competitions. Recently, I've tapped into the cycling scene, which is thriving, oddly, given the frankly unacceptable number of hills here in San Marcos.

This portfolio project just beginning, and I hope to expand on it in the coming months. I feel that the work I've done as a student is compelling. Ultimately, I am enthusiastic to contribute my unique gifts and talents to the industry.

I can be reached by email at: sds158@txstate.edu

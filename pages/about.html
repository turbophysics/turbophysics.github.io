About Me

Hi, my name is Sohail. I'm a USMC veteran, first gen American, art/film/literature/dog/biking/oxford comma enthusiast, and Computer Science student at Texas State University. I put this site together to showcase some of the more interesting work I've done during my attendance there.

I'm absolutely passionate about engineering functional software solutions in virtually any context, from UX design to biometrics or even the growing field of GIS. I believe my education at Texas State has been hollistic, and I've witnessed multiple novel applications for software solutions.

That said, I believe there is still great room for optimization in industrial level manufacturing applications. I spent a year working for a very competitive global manufacturer as an electromechanical technician. In my station I was responsible for recovering from failures, emergent situations, and assisting in preventing similar stoppages in the future; but in my capacity I could not address larger more complex problems. I saw first-hand how far even basic level software could produce incredible gains in consistency, throughput, and quality control; most of all, what I saw was how much room for improvement there was. The future may lie in quantom computing or sophisticated AI, but my experience has shown me that there is still much work to be done on the ground floor of our basic industries.

This portfolio project just beginning, and I hope to expand on it in the coming months. I feel that the work I've done as a student is compelling. I'm currently (as of 12/22/2020) working on overhauling this website to an Angular CLI based web app. Ultimately, I am enthusiastic to contribute my unique gifts and talents to the industry.

I can be reached by email at: sds158@txstate.edu
